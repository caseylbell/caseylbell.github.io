Master Pages


Step 1: Open Adobe InDesign & Name Your Project

Open Adobe InDesign. Then, name your Adobe InDesign project "master_pages.indd" and set your project width to 800px & height to 600px, and make sure that "Facing Pages" is not selected.

Step 2: Place The Photo Placeholder Image

Place the "photo-placeholder" image on your A-Master page (pictured).

Step 3: Open The Buttons And Forms Panel

Select "Window", then "Interactive", then ensure "Buttons and Forms" has a checkmark next to it; this will make your Buttons and Forms panel pop up. Then, in the Pages panel create four pages that all apply A-Master.

On your A-Master page, create four type boxes with the following names: (1) About Me, (2) Education, (3) Experience, and (4) Skills.

Step 4: Create Buttons

Still on your A-Master page, use the type boxes you created in the previous step to create four type buttons all with the action of "Go To Page" selected (pictured), the Event kept as "On Release or Tap", and using the following button names: (1) about, (2) education, (3) experience, and (4) skills.

Step 5: Make Sure You Successfully Created Your Buttons

You will know that you successfully created buttons when you see a button icon appear within each type box:

Step 6: Make Sure You Set Up Your Pages Panel Correctly

Your Pages panel should now have the following appearance:

Step 7: Add Placeholder Text

Still on your A-Master page, create two more type boxes, and fill them with either Placeholder Text for the time being (to add Placeholder Text easily, select "Type", then ensure "Fill with Placeholder Text" has a checkmark next to it) or personalized text.

Step 8: Customize The Pages

To begin customizing your four pages with your own words in place of placeholder text and your own visual design elements, in your Pages panel double click on a page to go into that specific page (in the provided image, Page 1 is selected).

Step 9: Use The Keyboard Shortcut To Customize Elements On Non-A-Master Pages

Once you are on the page that you wish to customize, on your keyboard press your Command (Mac)/CTRL (Windows) + Shift keys, and click on the specific element you wish to customize:
