Layers

Part 1

Step 1: Open The Image & Select Its Subject

Open your image in Adobe Photoshop.

On the Background layer, use the Lasso Tool or Object Selection Tool & object subtraction (On Mac computers, use the Option key to subtract from your selection)/addition (On Mac computers, use the Shift key to add to your selection), to select the subject in a concise manner:

Step 2: Create A New Layer Containing Your Selection

Press "Command+J" on your keyboard to create a new layer containing your selection; this new layer will appear above your Background layer:

Step 3: Copy/Paste The Layer

Copy this new layer in your "first_step.psd" project, then paste the layer into your "final_product.psd" project. Then, on Layer 1 and with the move tool selected, move the subject to your desired location.

Step 4: Rename The Layer

With Layer 1 selected, double click on the name "Layer 1" to rename your layer to "Subject":

Step 5: Open The Adjustments Panel

With the "Subject" layer selected, go to the "Adjustments" tab in your properties panel, and select the "Color Balance" adjustment type.

Step 6: Adjust The Color Balance

With the "Color Balance" layer selected, and the "Midtones" option selected within this panel, adjust the properties of the Yellows/Blues to "+15":

Step 7: Export Your Project As A JPEG File

Go to "File", "Export", "Export As" then export your finished project to your computer as a JPEG file.
