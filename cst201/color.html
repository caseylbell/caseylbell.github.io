Adobe Color

Part 1

Tool To Use:

Color Blind Safe Tool





Step 1: Open The Color Blind Safe Tool

Open the Color Blind Safe Tool, and look in the top right corner to make sure you are signed into your Adobe Cloud account:

Step 2: Select The Color Mode

Select "RGB" as your Color Mode:

Step 3: Adjust Your Colors

Adjust your colors by dragging each circle on the color wheel (each circle corresponds to either color A, B, C, D, or E within your color palette) until there is no "Potential Color Conflict":

Step 4: Observe The Color Blind Simulator

The "Color Blind Simulator" shows you how your colors will appear to people who have any of the three types of color blindness--Deuteranopia, Protanopia, or Tritanopia:

Step 5: Save Your Color Blind Safe Theme

Save your Color Blind Safe Theme to your Library:

Step 6: Make Sure You Successfully Saved

Upon successful saving, you will see this notification pop up in the bottom right corner of your screen:

Step 7: View Your Theme

View your Color Blind Safe Theme in 'Your Library':

Step 8: Open The CC Libraries Panel

Open Adobe InDesign, and select "Window" from InDesign’s top menu; under "Window" select "CC Libraries", and upon selecting CC Libraries, make sure a check mark pops up (indicating that your CC Libraries panel is now available to you).

Step 9: Search For Your Color Blind Safe Theme

Search for your Color Blind Safe Theme within your CC Libraries panel until it presents itself:

Step 10: Add Your Theme To Your Swatches

Right click on your Color Blind Safe Theme, and select "Add theme to swatches" from the dropdown menu:

Step 11: Open The Swatches Panel

Select "Window" from InDesign’s top menu again; under "Window" select "Color", then select "Swatches":

Step 12: Search For Your Color Blind Safe Theme

Within your Swatches panel, you should see your Color Blind Safe Theme:


Part 2

Tool To Use:

Contrast Checker Tool





Step 1: Open The Contrast Checker Tool & Color Blind Safe Theme

Open The Contrast Checker Tool. Then, in a separate window/tab, open your Color Blind Safe Theme from Part 1 of this lab, and hover over any of your colors within this color palette to Copy the color code for that color:

Step 2: Paste The Color Code

Paste the color code you just copied into either the "Text Color" or the "Background Color" area:

Step 3: Repeat Step Two

Repeat Part 2 Step 2 of this lab, but select a color you think would contrast best with the initial color you selected, then paste its color code into either the Text Color (if you used your initial color for your background color) or the Background Color (if you used your initial color for your text color).

Make sure that Contrast Ratio has a green checkmark to its right; if you do not have enough contrast between your two colors (in this case you will see a red circle with a line through it), go back to your Color Blind Safe Theme and select colors that will contrast better with each other, then continue to repeat this lab until your Contrast Ratio is high enough to satisfy at least WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards (Level AA is the default level used by Adobe Color).
