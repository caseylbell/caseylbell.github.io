Masking

Part 1

Step 1: Open The Images & Set Up Your Workspace

Open your images in Adobe Photoshop. To set up your workspace, drag the "background" image out of the project area.

Step 2: Place The "background" Image On Top Of The "portrait" Image

Step 3: Select The Masking Tool

Select the Masking tool within your layers panel.

Step 4: Paint On The Layer Mask Thumbnail With The Brush Tool

Press "Command+I" on a Mac keyboard to invert the layer mask thumbnail from a white fill to a black fill; make sure your selected brush color is white, make sure your Flow is set to 15%, and mask it up!


Part 2

Step 1: Open The Images In Camera Raw & Adjust Settings

Open your images in Adobe Photoshop. In Camera Raw, adjust the Highlights and Color Mixer settings:

Step 2: Open The Images In Photoshop & Select The Hat

Open the images; for the "hat" image, select the hat using object selection or the lasso tool, and press "Command+J" on your Mac keyboard to place this selection on a new layer. Then, repeat these tasks for the "subject" image.

Step 3: Adjust The Hat Shape & Size

Copy and paste the newly created hat object/layer on top of the "subject" image, to allow yourself to see what proportions you should use for the hat (in comparison to the size of the subject); for the "hat" image, press "Command+T" on your Mac keyboard to transform the hat; then, while still in transform mode, right click on the hat and Warp.

Step 4: Create A New Project

Now, create a new project, paste the new "hat" and "subject" objects/layers into this project, and title this new project; add a background color to your workspace by using the Rectangle tool and ensuring this new layer is below all other layers (but still above the base "Background" layer).
